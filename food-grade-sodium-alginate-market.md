<p><p>The food grade sodium alginate market can be categorized into two main types: powder and granular. Powdered sodium alginate is finely ground and often used in applications requiring quick dissolution, such as sauces, dressings, and gel formulations. Granular sodium alginate has a coarser texture, making it suitable for applications with longer mixing times, like baked goods and thickening agents. Both types serve critical roles in the food industry, contributing to texture, stability, and shelf life of various products.</p></p>
